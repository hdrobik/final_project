What is public art?

Art created for placement in the public space and presented there. He has been present since the beginning of the history of art. It has many functions: decorative, commemorative, propaganda, etc. It uses various materials and takes various forms. These can be, for example, frescoes, mosaics, sculptures (friezes, monuments, etc.), stained glass windows. Public art is sometimes described as an alternative to the elite art of the gallery. This understanding appeared in the 1960s, and its purpose was defined as the shaping and revitalization of the city's space. With time, the most important feature of public art has become the possibility of dialogue with the recipient, as well as with exhibition spaces. Unsplash!
